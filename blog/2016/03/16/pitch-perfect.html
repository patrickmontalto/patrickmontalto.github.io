Pitch Perfect

I began Udacity’s iOS program yesterday to explore how in-depth it got with certain things that were being left out of Rob Percival’s Udemy iOS 9/Swift 2.0 course.

I am glad to say that I am pleasantly surprised to find there to be a good deal of interaction and good habit-forming techniques promoted. Although I am already quite familiar with Swift through BNR’s book and have built about 5-10 small apps through Treehouse and Udemy, it is refreshing to be in such a constructive learning environment like Udacity.

Here’s a summary of what I’ve done at Udacity my first day:

Import audio files Import images Play audio files Stop audio files change audio playback speed (rate) Reset audio playback position (reset to beginning) ViewController lifecycle This was the biggest thing so far, all of the aforementioned stuff I had already learned from Udemy or Treehouse. Some neat shortcuts in Xcode (hiding navigator/utilities, etc) All in all it was a good first day.
